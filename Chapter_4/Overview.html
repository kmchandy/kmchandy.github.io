State Transition Systems

Chapter Overview

This chapter describes state-transition systems and shows how to prove safety and progress by using predicates on states. The chapter has modules that review predicates and Hoare triples. Skim through or skip these modules if you are familiar with these concepts.

The running example module gives an example of a system that we use to illustrate ideas. The module on state-transition systems describes a model of systems defined in terms of labeled graphs. The modules on how to prove safety and how to prove progress give proof techniques that are often used to prove safety and progress; however, these are not the only techniques we use. The module on helpful formulae gives a list of formulae that are self-evident and with simple proofs. We will refer to this list when we design algorithms.
