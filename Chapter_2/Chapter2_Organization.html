Chapter Overview: Concepts in Temporal Logic

This chapter has two modules, Safety, and Progress, that introduce all the concepts that we use to prove that systems behave correctly.

Module on Safety

This module introduces all the concepts that we use in this course to prove that systems are safe. The concepts are defined in terms of directed graphs.

Module on Progress

A system that remains for ever in a safe state, and does nothing, is a safe system; however, it is not useful. We want to prove that system trajectories progress towards their goals. This module introduces all the concepts that we use to prove that system trajectories progress. The concepts are described in terms of labeled directed graphs..

A later chapter describes the same concepts in terms of predicates and states of systems.
