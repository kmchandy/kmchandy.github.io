Computations: Review

Answer these questions to review the webpage on computations.

A computation is a central concept in describing and analyzing distributed algorithms. The relationship between computations and dataflow helps us understand many algorithms.

  1. What is the relationship between a single event and an iteration of the sequential program abstraction of a distributed system.
  2. What is the change in state caused by execution of a single iteration of the while loop representation of a distributed system? What is the relationship between this change in state and the change in state specified by an event?
  3. What is a computation?
  4. What is the relationship between a computation and the sequence of states of the while loop abstraction of a distributed system?
  5. Review sequential programming: What is an invariant of a while loop? How do you prove invariants?
  6. Review sequential programming: What is a loop variant of a while loop?
  7. Review sequential programming: Review your earlier courses on proving properties of loops. Give and prove an algorithm for computing the all-points shortest paths in graphs.
