Past and Future in the Model

Only Notion of Time: Before and After

The only notions of time in a dataflow graph are the partial orders before and after. An event \(e\) is before an event \(e'\), and \(e'\) is after \(e\), in a dataflow graph exactly when there is a path from \(e\) to \(e'\).

Slice: Partition Event Set into Past and Future

A slice of a dataflow graph is a partition of the set of events of the graph into subsets past and future such that all events before past events are past events.

An equivalent definition is: All events after future events are future events.

Example

The top diagram of figure 1 shows a slice in which vertices in past are colored red and vertices in future are green. The curved black line is the boundary separating past from future. All edges to past events are from past events. All edges from future events are to future events. The state of the system at a slice is specified by the events from past to future.
A Slice Partitions a Dataflow Graphs into Two Dataflow Graphs
Associated with a slice of a dataflow graph \(G\) are two graphs called the past and future graphs. The events (vertices) of the past graph are past events in \(G\) with an additional final event for each agent. The events of the future graph are future events in \(G\) with an additional initial event for each agent.

The edges to the final event for an agent u in the past graph are the edges to events at u in \(G\) from past to future. The edges from the initial event for an agent v in the future graph are the edges from events at v in \(G\) from past to future .

The events of the past graph are identical to those in \(G\) except for the addition of final vertices. Because \(G\) is a dataflow graph of the system it follows that the past graph is also a dataflow graph of the system. Likewise the future graph is a dataflow graph of the system.

Example

The lower diagram of figure 1 shows two graphs, the red one consisting of events in past and the green one consisting of events in future. Vertices representing final states of past and initial states of future are added to complete the graphs. Vertices and edges in the original dataflow graph (upper diagram) are identical to the corresponding vertices and edges in the past and future graphs (lower diagram).
The Final State of Past is the Initial State of Future
The edges that cross the boundary from past to future in the dataflow graph \(G\) are the edges to the final vertices of the past dataflow graph; these are also the edges from the initial vertices of the future dataflow graph. So, the final state of the past dataflow graph is the same as the initial state of the future dataflow graph. This state is called the state at the slice.

Let S and S' be the initial and final states, respectively, of a dataflow of a system and let S* be the state at a slice of the dataflow.

There exists dataflows of the system from S to S* and from S* to S'.

Any topological sort of the dataflow graph is a computation. So, there exists computations from S to S* and from S* to S'
