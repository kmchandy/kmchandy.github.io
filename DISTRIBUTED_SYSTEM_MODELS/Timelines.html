Past and Future in Dataflow

Recall that a vertex \(v\) is before a vertex \(v'\) in the dataflow graph exactly when there is a path from \(v\) to \(v'\). And \(v'\) is after \(v\) exactly when \(v\) is before \(v'\).

A slice of a dataflow graph is a partition of the set of vertices of the graph into subsets past and future such that all events before past events are past events, or equivalently all events after future events are future events.

The state of the system at a slice is specified by the labels of edges from past to future.

Example

The top diagram of figure 1 shows a slice in which vertices in past are colored red and vertices in future are green. The curved black line is the boundary separating past from future. In figure 1 all edges to past events are from past events, and all edges from future events are to future events.

The state S* after completion of past events and before initiation of future events is given by the labels of edges from past to future. These are the edges that cross the boundary line separating past from future.

S* is the final state of the sub-dataflow of past events. S* is also the initial state of the sub-dataflow of future events.

See the lower diagram in figure 1. S* is shown as final vertices (which are labeled N) of past dataflow, and S* is shown as as initial vertices (which are labeled 0) of future dataflow.

Reachability of States at Slices

For a dataflow graph with initial state \(S_{init}\) and final state \(S_{fini}\) and a state \(S^{*}\) at a slice, there exists a dataflow of
  1. past events with initial state \(S_{init}\) and final state \(S^{*}\), and
  2. future events with initial state \(S*\) and final state \(S_{fini}\).

Theorem

Given a dataflow graph and a slice (past, future) of the graph, there exists a computation that starts at the initial state of the dataflow, and executes the events in past and then executes events in future.

Proof

There exists a topological sort of the dataflow graph where vertices in past appear before vertices in future.

Points in an Agent's Computation in a Dataflow

The computation of an agent \(a\) in a dataflow is the sequence of steps at agent \(a\) in the computation. It is given by the horizontal line representing steps of the agent in the dataflow diagram.

A point in the computation of an agent \(a\) in a dataflow is an integer \(k\) and identifies the edge from the \(k\)-th to the \(k+1\)-th step of \(a\).

Theorem

Given a dataflow graph \(G\) and a point \(n_{a}\) in the computation of each agent \(a\) in \(G\):

There is a slice (past, future) where past consists of the first \(n_{a}\) steps of agent \(a\) exactly when:

Every message received in past is sent in past,
Equivalently:
Every message sent in future is received in future

Proof

Consider an edge \(v', v\) in the dataflow graph where \(v\) is in past. If the edge is between successive steps at the same agent \(a\) then \(v'\) is also in past because the first \(n_{a}\) steps of \(a\) are in past, and if \(v\) is one of the first \(n_{a}\) steps then so is \(v'\).

If the edge is a message edge where \(v'\) is a step in which a message is sent and \(v\) is the step in which that message is received, then the condition of the observation implies that \(v'\) is also in past.

Observation
For a channel \(c\), let \(c_{sent}\) and \(c_{rcvd}\) be the numbers of messages sent and received along the channel at each point in a computation.

Given a dataflow graph \(G\) and a point \(n_{a}\) in the computation of each agent \(a\) in \(G\): There is a slice (past, future) where past consists of the first \(n_{a}\) steps of agent \(a\) exactly when: \(c_{sent} \geq c_{rcvd}\).
