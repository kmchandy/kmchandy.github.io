Properties of Dataflow: FAQ

Why is the state at a slice defined only for closed slices?/p> The state at a closed slice is defined as the state after executing all events in the past at the slice and before executing any event in the future.

There exists a computation in which all events in the past are executed before events in the future because the past is closed -- an example of such a computation is a topological sort of the past followed by a topological sort of the future.

If the past is not closed there is no computation that executes all the events in the past before executing events in the future.
