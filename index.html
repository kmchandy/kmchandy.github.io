Distributed Algorithms Contents Index
This website shows that algorithms used in distributed systems are based on a few simple ideas.

Distributed systems consists of agents that communicate with each other by sending and receiving messages. Diverse applications are based on message-passing agents. Cryptocurrencies are based on agents that both compete and collaborate by sending and receiving messages. Autonomous vehicles gather information sent by a variety of sensors such as lidar, radar, GPS, and accelerometers. The power grid is a distributed system with information flowing from power generators energy markets, and substations. Multiplayer games are based on message-passing among agents.

This website shows that the diversity of distributed applications are based on a small number of concepts. The website has implementations of algorithms in Python, Golang, and other programming languages, using protocols such as AMQP and AWS Simple Queue Service (SQS) are given. Proofs of correctness of algorithms are given using UNITY .

Some of the material in this website has been used in a course taken by undergraduates even in their first year. Elementary graph theory is the only prerequisite.

Each page in this website begins with the central idea underlying a set of algorithms. The page ends with a summary of the central idea and how it is used in algorithm design. The website shows that algorithms for new problems can be designed systematically by combining a small number of basic ideas.

K. Mani Chandy, Emeritus Simon Ramo Professor, California Institute of Technology
