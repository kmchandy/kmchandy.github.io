Distributed Algorithms

This website has short, self-contained descriptions of distributed algorithms. It also has supporting videos, examples, self tests, and longer descriptions. The material is used in an undergraduate class taken by students with an elementary knowledge of graphs. Informal descriptions allow students to understand key ideas quickly while ancillary material provides proofs and details.

The course emphasizes that methods used to design sequential and distributed algorithms are similar. The computation model of distributed systems is the UNITY model which is an incremental extension of the nondeterministic loop. The descriptions of many distributed algorithms are based on the familiar loop.

The descriptions of some algorithms require fairness and a temporal logic. These descriptions use UNITY logic because it is simple and is is defined in terms of the universal fragment of the widely-used Computation Tree Logic. Algorithm descriptions have enough detail to allow readers to construct formal proofs.
