Exercise Module
Properties of Relations

This module lists properties of relations that we use frequently. You can treat this module as the following exercise: identify useful properties about \(\subseteq\) and then determine whether similar properties hold for \(\rightarrow\), \(\stackrel{*}{\rightarrow}\) and \(\leadsto\).

The properties are obvious, and you can also skip or skim this module.

Terminology: When \(P \subset Q\) we say that "\(P\) is stronger than \(Q\)" and "\(Q\) is weaker than \(P\)" because we know more about \(P\) than we do about \(Q\). For example, we know more about Maya if we know that she is a CS student than if all we know is that she is a student.

The properties of \(\rightarrow\), \(\stackrel{*}{\rightarrow}\), and \(\leadsto\) are similar to those of \(\subseteq\) with which you are already familiar. We show the properties of \(\subseteq\) to emphasize this similarity.

Reflexive

  1. \( P \subseteq P \)
  2. \( P \leadsto P \)

Strengthen Left-Hand Side

  1. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (Q \subseteq R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \subseteq R) \)

  2. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (Q \rightarrow R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \rightarrow R) \)

    If all successors of \(Q\) are in \(R\) then all successors of subsets of \(Q\) are in \(R\).

  3. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (Q \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} R) \)

    If all vertices reachable from \(Q\) are in \(R\) then all vertices reachable from subsets of \(Q\) are in \(R\).

  4. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (Q \leadsto R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \leadsto R) \)

    If every fair path from every vertex in \(Q\) visits a vertex in \(R\) then every fair path from every vertex in subsets of \(Q\) visits a vertex in \(R\).

Weaken Right-Hand Side

  1. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (Q \subseteq R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \subseteq R) \)

  2. \( (P \rightarrow Q) \wedge (Q \subseteq R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \rightarrow R) \)

    If all successors of \(P\) are in \(Q\) then they are also in supersets of \(Q\).

  3. \( (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} Q) \wedge (Q \subseteq R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} R) \)

    If all vertices reachable from \(P\) are in \(Q\) then they are also in supersets of \(Q\).

  4. \( (P \leadsto Q) \wedge (Q \subseteq R) \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \leadsto R) \)

    If every path from \(P\) visits a vertex in \(Q\) then it also visits a vertex in supersets of \(Q\).

Union Left-Hand Side

  1. \( (P \subseteq R) \wedge (Q \subseteq R) \quad \equiv \quad (P \cup Q) \subseteq R \)

  2. \( (P \rightarrow R) \wedge (Q \rightarrow R) \quad \equiv \quad (P \cup Q) \rightarrow R \)

    All successors of \(P\) and of \(Q\) are in \(R\) exactly when all successors of the union of \(P\) and \(Q\) are in \(R\)

  3. \( (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} R) \wedge (Q \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} R) \quad \equiv \quad (P \cup Q) \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} R \)

    All vertices reachable from \(P\) and from \(Q\) are in \(R\) exactly when all vertices reachable from \(P \cup Q\) are in \(R\).

  4. \( (P \leadsto R) \wedge (Q \leadsto R) \quad \equiv \quad (P \cup Q) \leadsto R \)

    Every fair path from every vertex in \(P\) and from every vertex in \(Q\) visits \(R\).

Intersection Right-Hand Side

  1. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (P \subseteq R) \quad \equiv \quad P \subseteq (Q \cap R) \)

  2. \( (P \rightarrow Q) \wedge (P \rightarrow R) \quad \equiv \quad P \rightarrow (Q \cap R) \)

    All successors of \(P\) are in \(Q\) and also in \(R\) exactly when they are in the intersection of \(Q\) and \(R\).

  3. \( (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} Q) \wedge (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} R) \quad \equiv \quad P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} (Q \cap R) \)

    All vertices reachable from \(P\) are in \(Q\) and also in \(R\) exactly when they are in the intersection of \(Q\) and \(R\).

  4. For stable \(Q\) and \(R\):

    \( (P \leadsto Q) \wedge (P \leadsto R) \quad \equiv \quad P \leadsto (Q \cap R) \)

    Every fair path from every vertex in \(P\) visits and remains forever in \(Q\) and also visits and remains forever in \(R\) exactly when it visits and remains forever in the intersection of \(Q\) and \(R\).

    If \(Q\) and \(R\) are not stable there can be a fair path from a vertex in \(P\) that visits \(Q\) and then visits \(R\), but never visits \(Q\) and \(R\) at the same instant.

Conjunction of Relations

  1. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (P' \subseteq Q') \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \cap P') \subseteq (Q \cap Q') \)
  2. \( (P \rightarrow Q) \wedge (P' \rightarrow Q') \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \cap P') \rightarrow (Q \cap Q') \)
  3. \( (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} Q) \wedge (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} Q') \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \cap P') \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} (Q \cap Q') \)

  4. For stable \(Q\) and \(Q'\):

    \( (P \leadsto Q) \wedge (P' \leadsto Q') \quad \equiv \quad (P \cap P') \leadsto (Q \cap Q') \)

Disjunction of Relations

  1. \( (P \subseteq Q) \wedge (P' \subseteq Q') \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \cup P') \subseteq (Q \cup Q') \)
  2. \( (P \rightarrow Q) \wedge (P' \rightarrow Q') \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \cup P') \rightarrow (Q \cup Q') \)
  3. \( (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} Q) \wedge (P \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} Q') \quad \Rightarrow \quad (P \cup P') \stackrel{*}{\rightarrow} (Q \cup Q') \)
  4. \( (P \leadsto Q) \wedge (P' \leadsto Q') \quad \equiv \quad (P \cup P') \leadsto (Q \cup Q') \)

Summary

All the properties, except for the intersection of the right-hand sides for \(\leadsto\), are obvious; they are listed here merely for completeness.
