Foundations Of Distributed Systems

This chapter describes concepts at the foundation of distributed systems. These concepts are extensions of concepts in sequential programming.

Agents and Channels as Vertices and Edges

Distributed systems are defined in terms of their states and state transitions. Safety shows how states and state transitions are represented as vertices and edges of directed graphs.

Safety

We have to prove that distributed systems remain forever in safe states. Safety shows that we prove this by showing that safety is invariant: Initial states are safe and all transitions from safe states are to safe states.

Distributed systems are defined as sets of agents and communication channels between agents. Distributed Systems Introduction discusses representation of agents and channels as vertices and edges, respectively, of directed graphs.

System Timelines

Timelines show how the state of a system evolves over time. Timelines are a useful concept in designing distributed algorithms.

Progress: Introduction

We prove that trajectories or computations of distributed systems progress towards their goals by using a concept, called leads to from temporal logic. Progress defines this concept and discusses how we use CTL (computation tree logic) to prove correctness. The emphasis throughout this website is on the similarity of concepts in sequential and distributed algorithms, and we show how concepts such as invariants and variant functions are used to prove safety and progress.

Rules to Prove Progress

Progress Properties gives three simple rules that we use to prove that distributed systems progress towards their goals.
