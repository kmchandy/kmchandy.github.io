Foundations Of Distributed Systems

This chapter describes foundational concepts of distributed systems.

The module on agents and channels describes:

  1. the specification of distributed systems as directed graph in which vertices represent agents and edges represent channels, and
  2. computations of distributed system defined as executions of nondeterministic loops described in the previous chapter.

The next module on timelines shows how the computation of a distributed system can be defined in terms of a timeline that shows the states of component agents and channels as time progresses.
