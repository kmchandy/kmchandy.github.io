Review of Basics

Programmers use invariants, and loop variants to design both sequential and distributed algorithms. This section is a review of these basic concepts.

The section reviews nondeterministic iteration in the form of Dijkstra's do-od loop. Nondeterminism in distributed algorithms is an extension of nondeterministic iterations used in sequential programs.

The section also reviews predicates and shows the relation between predicates and sets of states of a system. We review Hoare triples to show the relationship between programs and predicates This section includes a review of invariants and loop variants, also called variant functions, which are used to prove that an iteration terminates. The section closes with examples of proofs of correctness of algorithms.
