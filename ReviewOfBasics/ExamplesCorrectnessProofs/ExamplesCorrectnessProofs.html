Self Tests: Examples of Correctness Proofs

This section consists of self tests of correctness proofs. You can use these tests to check your knowledge of nondeterministic iteration and:
  1. Invariants
  2. Variant functions
  3. Predicate that holds upon termination
The self tests cover the following problems:
  1. Sorting
  2. Shortest paths in a graph
  3. Distributed averaging
  4. Formation of objects in flight
  5. Earliest meeting time of concurrent agents
You will see that proofs of correctness of distributed and sequential algorithms are much the same. Concepts in sequential algorithms also appear in reasoning about distributed systems with continuous states that evolve over continuous time.
