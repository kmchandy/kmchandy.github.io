Review: Sequential Programming

Sequential algorithms are the foundation for distributed algorithms. Similar methods are used to design and reason about both sequential and distributed algorithms.

Nondeterministic iteration, in the form of Dijkstra's do-od loop, is a central concept in both sequential and distributed algorithms. The model of concurrent computation used in this course is UNITY, an extension of the do-od loop. We reason about both sequential and distributed algorithms by analyzing computations - sequences of program states.

The chapter reviews states, nondeterministic iteration , predicates, Hoare triples , invariants , loop variants , and safety in state-transition systems --- concepts that are central to sequential and concurrent programming.
